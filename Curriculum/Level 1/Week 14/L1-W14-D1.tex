\begin{instructbox}
\textbf{Use the Addition Partner} — instead of counting back 6 or 7 steps, ask yourself the addition question:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{$-6$:} \quad $10-6=?$ \;\to\; ``6 plus what makes 10?'' \quad $6+4=10$, so $10-6=4$
  \item \textbf{$-7$:} \quad $9-7=?$ \;\to\; ``7 plus what makes 9?'' \quad $7+2=9$, so $9-7=2$
\end{itemize}
\textit{Knowing addition facts makes subtraction fast!}
\end{instructbox}

\bigskip
\noindent{\color{sd-blue}\large\textbf{Part I — Taking Away Six}} \hfill \textit{(Problems 1–8)}

\medskip\noindent\textit{Think of the addition partner. Write the difference or missing number.}

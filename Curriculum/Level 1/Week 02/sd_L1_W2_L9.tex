\lessonheader{2}{9}{Comparing Numbers 0–10}

\begin{instructbox}
\noindent\textbf{\color{sd-blue}Instruction} \textit{(read aloud):}\\[4pt]
We already know \textbf{more}, \textbf{fewer}, and \textbf{same} for small numbers.
Now we use those words for numbers all the way to ten.
The bigger the number, the more objects in the group.
\end{instructbox}

\noindent{\color{sd-blue}$\bigstar$ \textbf{Enrichment — Mayan Numbers:}}\\
The Maya counted using dots and bars.
A dot $\bullet$ = 1. A bar \rule{0.8cm}{2pt} = 5.\\[4pt]
\begin{tabular}{ccccccc}

## PERSONS — Know who each person is and what they did

- **Leif Erikson** — Norse explorer who reached North America centuries before Columbus
- **Ferdinand and Isabella** — Spanish monarchs who funded Columbus's voyages
- **Christopher Columbus** — Italian explorer sailing for Spain; first voyage reached San Salvador, 1492
- **John Smith** — Captain who saved Jamestown by requiring colonists to work for food
- **Pocahontas** — Powhatan girl who helped at Jamestown; later married John Rolfe
- **Lord De La Warr** — Royal governor who arrived with reinforcements and saved Jamestown from collapse
- **John Rolfe** — Grew tobacco in Virginia; married Pocahontas
- **William Bradford** — Pilgrim governor; wrote a journal of Plymouth's founding
- **Massasoit** — Wampanoag leader who made a peace treaty with the Plymouth colonists
- **John Winthrop** — Puritan leader; said Massachusetts Bay would be "a city upon a hill"

## TERMS AND TOPICS — Know what each term means

- **caravel** — a fast, nimble sailing ship used by Portuguese and Spanish explorers
- **merchants** — traders who buy and sell goods; helped fund exploration
- **nation-states** — powerful countries with a central government; competed to claim new lands
- *Niña*, *Pinta*, and *Santa María* — Columbus's three ships on his first voyage
- **Taíno** — the Native people Columbus first met at San Salvador
- **conquistadors** — Spanish conquerors who explored and claimed land in the Americas
- **Columbian Exchange** — the transfer of peoples, plants, animals, and diseases between Old and New Worlds
- **smallpox** — European disease that devastated Native American populations
- **Virginia Company** — English trading company that funded and founded Jamestown
- **indentured servants** — workers who traded years of labor for passage to America and shelter
- **"Starving Time"** — terrible winter at Jamestown (1609–1610) when many colonists starved
- **tobacco** — cash crop that made Virginia profitable
- **Powhatan** — the confederacy of Native peoples surrounding Jamestown; also the name of their chief
- **House of Burgesses** — first elected assembly in the American colonies (Virginia, 1619)
- **Pilgrims** — Separatists who sailed on the *Mayflower* seeking religious freedom
- *Mayflower* — the ship that brought the Pilgrims to Plymouth in 1620
- **Mayflower Compact** — document the Pilgrims signed agreeing to govern themselves under just laws
- **religious freedom** — the right to worship as one chooses
- **self-government** — the people having a say in the laws they live under
- **Wampanoag** — Native people of the Plymouth region; made peace with the Pilgrims
- **Puritans** — English Protestants who founded Massachusetts Bay Colony

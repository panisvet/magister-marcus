# *The New World Made Ready* — Unit 1
## Weekly Pacing Schedule
### The British Colonies of North America, 1492–1763
**4 days/week · 9 weeks · ~36 lesson sessions**

---

## How to Use This Schedule

- **Day 1** is typically geography, context-setting, or map work.
- **Day 2** is the core history narrative lesson — the "story day." This is the heart of the week.
- **Day 3** brings in a primary source, biography study, or supplemental resource (video, art, living book chapter).
- **Day 4** is student written work: worksheet, notebooking page, or quiz/review.

**Sessions run 45–60 minutes.** Geography and narrative days can run longer if discussion is rich — let it. Written work days are typically 30–45 minutes.

**Read-alouds** are assigned as ongoing alongside lessons. Aim for 2–3 chapters of the current read-aloud per week, as a separate reading time (not counted in the 4 lesson days).

## WEEK 1
**Lesson 1 — The Land and Its Waters**
*Focus: Topographic geography of North America, east to west*

| Day | Activity | Resources |
|-----|----------|-----------|
| **Day 1** | **Intro: What Is a Map?** Read aloud *Maps and Globes* (Knowlton). Introduce map vs. globe. Practice cardinal directions. | *Maps and Globes*; printed blank North America outline map |
| **Day 2** | **Geography Narrative: East to West.** Teacher-narrated journey from the Atlantic coast to the Pacific: Atlantic Ocean → Appalachian Mountains → Great Plains → Rocky Mountains → Pacific Ocean. Students add features to their maps. | Blank outline map; imagery of key landforms |
| **Day 3** | **Geography Narrative: Rivers, Lakes, and the Gulf.** Mississippi River, Great Lakes, Gulf of Mexico, Bering Strait. Discussion: Why do rivers matter to settlers? Where would you want to live? | Reference maps; David Rumsey collection image (printed) |
| **Day 4** | **Map Work & Worksheet.** Students label their blank maps from memory using a word bank. Geography Worksheet 1 (Lesson 1). | Worksheet U1-GEO-1 |

**Read-Aloud This Week:** Begin *The Landing of the Pilgrims* (Daugherty), Ch. 1–2 (separate from lesson time)

## WEEK 2
**Lesson 1 — The Peoples of America**
*Focus: Native peoples of North America before European contact*

| Day | Activity | Resources |
|-----|----------|-----------|
| **Day 1** | **Native Civilizations: An Overview.** Maya, Aztec, Inca — where they were, how they lived. Show photographs of ruins. Return to the North American map and place major eastern tribes. | Images from LOC or Britannica; student map |
| **Day 2** | **Story Day: Columbus's Crew Sights Land.** Teacher narrates the scene: October 12, 1492. The long weeks at sea, the near-mutiny, the birds, the cry — *"¡Tierra!"* Discussion questions. | Teacher narrative notes (Lesson 1 plan) |
| **Day 3** | **Supplemental: Native Life and European Life Compared.** What was daily life like for a Wampanoag child? For an English child in 1600? Students discuss differences and similarities. View 1–2 Britannica Kids articles or Colonial Williamsburg video. | Britannica Kids; CW video "Native People of Virginia" |
| **Day 4** | **Notebooking Page 1 — The Land of America.** Students complete notebooking page: sketch a landform, write description, answer reflection question. | Notebooking Page U1-NB-1 |

**Read-Aloud This Week:** *The Landing of the Pilgrims*, Ch. 3–4

## WEEK 3
**Lesson 2 — Columbus and the Age of Exploration**
*Focus: Columbus; other explorers; why Europeans came west*

| Day | Activity | Resources |
|-----|----------|-----------|
| **Day 1** | **Context: Why Did Europeans Start Exploring?** Brief lesson on Muslim trade routes, Portuguese coast work, Spanish monarchy, new maritime technology. Map: Columbus's four voyages. | Exploration & Conquest (Maestro), Ch. 1–2; voyage map from LOC |
| **Day 2** | **Story Day: Columbus.** Full narrative of the first voyage, landfall on San Salvador, meeting the Taíno. What Columbus thought he had found. His later failures. Discussion: Was Columbus a success or a failure? | *Aboard the Santa Maria*; teacher narrative notes |
| **Day 3** | **Primary Source: Columbus's Letter (Excerpts).** Read teacher-paraphrased excerpts of Columbus's letter to Ferdinand and Isabella describing the New World. Students listen and respond. | Excerpts (teacher-prepared from public domain text); Animated Hero Classics — Columbus (video, 30 min.) |
| **Day 4** | **Worksheet — Lesson 2, Part A.** Columbus and early exploration. Form IA or IIA. | Worksheet U1-L2A |

**Read-Aloud:** *Exploration & Conquest* (Maestro), Ch. 3–5 (or continue *Pilgrims*)

---

## WEEK 4
**Lesson 2 — Roanoke, Jamestown, Plymouth**
*Focus: First English settlements; self-government begins*

| Day | Activity | Resources |
|-----|----------|-----------|
| **Day 1** | **Roanoke: The Lost Colony.** Story of England's first attempt. The mystery. What might have happened? Students speculate. | *Roanoke* (Yolen), read aloud; map of Roanoke Island |
| **Day 2** | **Story Day: Jamestown.** Virginia Company, John Smith, the Starving Time, Pocahontas, tobacco, John Rolfe. Discussion: What saved Jamestown? | *James Towne* (Sewall), Ch. 1–3; teacher narrative notes |
| **Day 3** | **Story Day: Plymouth and the Mayflower Compact.** ★ The voyage, the first winter, Massasoit and the Wampanoag, the First Thanksgiving. Primary source: Winslow's letter (1 paragraph). Mayflower Compact text — read together. ⚖️ | *The Thanksgiving Story* (Dalgliesh); Mayflower Compact full text; Winslow excerpt |
| **Day 4** | **★ Formative Quiz 1** (Lessons 1–2). Review sheet distributed day before. | Quiz U1-Q1 (Form IA or IIA) |

> **★ Midunit Test** may be administered at end of Week 4 or beginning of Week 5.

**Read-Aloud:** *The Landing of the Pilgrims* (Daugherty), Ch. 5–8 (complete book)

## WEEK 5
**Lesson 3 — The Colonies in Profile, Part I**
*Focus: New England and Middle colonies; founding stories; religious freedom*

| Day | Activity | Resources |
|-----|----------|-----------|
| **Day 1** | **Map Day: The 13 Colonies.** Distribute and label outline maps of the 13 colonies. Color-code the three regions: New England (blue), Middle (green), Southern (yellow). Discuss climate and geography differences. | 13 Colonies outline map (PCL or Grok-generated) |
| **Day 2** | **Story Day: New England Colonies.** Massachusetts Bay, Connecticut, Rhode Island, New Hampshire. Roger Williams and religious toleration. Winthrop's "city upon a hill." ⚖️ | Teacher narrative; *If You Lived in Colonial Times*, Ch. 1–3 |
| **Day 3** | **Story Day: Middle Colonies.** New York, New Jersey, Pennsylvania, Delaware. William Penn and the Quakers. Lord Baltimore and Maryland. Philadelphia. | Teacher narrative; *If You Lived in Colonial Times*, Ch. 4–5; *Benjamin West — Penn's Treaty* (image study) |
| **Day 4** | **Notebooking Page 2 — My Colony.** Students choose one colony, write its founding story, and draw a scene from colonial life there. | Notebooking Page U1-NB-2 |

**Read-Aloud:** *If You Lived in Colonial Times* (McGovern) — read 2–3 sections per sitting

## WEEK 6
**Lesson 3 — The Colonies in Profile, Part II**
*Focus: Southern colonies; colonial life; slavery and the Triangle Trade*

| Day | Activity | Resources |
|-----|----------|-----------|
| **Day 1** | **Story Day: Southern Colonies.** Virginia, North/South Carolina, Georgia, Maryland. James Oglethorpe and Georgia. Cash crops: tobacco, rice, indigo. | Teacher narrative; *If You Lived in Williamsburg* (Brenner), Ch. 1–3 |
| **Day 2** | **Story Day: Colonial Life.** What did a typical colonist's day look like? Food, clothing, shelter, schools, trades, church. Form IA: one family; Form IIA: compare colonial regions. | *If You Lived in Colonial Times* (reference); Colonial Williamsburg video resource |
| **Day 3** | **Hard History: Slavery and the Middle Passage.** History of slavery, origins in Africa and Portugal's sugar colonies, the Triangle Trade, the Middle Passage, first Africans at Jamestown. Stories of enslaved people and of those who won their freedom. ⚖️ | Britannica Kids "Transatlantic Slave Trade"; teacher narrative notes; Internet Archive images |
| **Day 4** | **★ Formative Quiz 2** (Lesson 3). | Quiz U1-Q2 (Form IA or IIA) |

**Read-Aloud:** *The New Americans* (Maestro, B.) OR *Three Young Pilgrims* (Harness)

## WEEK 7
**Lesson 4 — Major Events, Part I**
*Focus: English political heritage; colonial self-government; Great Awakening*

| Day | Activity | Resources |
|-----|----------|-----------|
| **Day 1** | **Political Background: Magna Carta → Glorious Revolution.** The English tradition of limiting royal power. What colonists knew and believed about rights. ⚖️ | British Library Magna Carta resources; teacher narrative |
| **Day 2** | **Story Day: Self-Government in the Colonies.** Colonial assemblies (Virginia House of Burgesses). Representation. Benign neglect — why England's inattention was actually good for the colonists. ⚖️ | Teacher narrative notes; Lesson 4 plan |
| **Day 3** | **The Great Awakening.** What it was, why it mattered. Jonathan Edwards, George Whitefield. How it gave colonists something in common across all 13 separate colonies. | Teacher narrative; short video clip (YouTube — search "Great Awakening for kids") |
| **Day 4** | **Biography Study: Young George Washington.** Boyhood, Rules of Civility, Virginia militia. Students copy 2 Rules of Civility (copywork) and write a sentence about each. | Biography Pages U1-BIO-GW; *Meet George Washington* (Heilbroner) |

**Read-Aloud:** *Ben Franklin of Old Philadelphia* (Cousins), Ch. 1–4

## WEEK 8
**Lesson 4 — Major Events, Part II**
*Focus: French and Indian War; Washington and Franklin; Treaty of Paris*

| Day | Activity | Resources |

| **Day 2** | **Story Day: The French and Indian War.** Young Washington at Fort Duquesne; General Braddock's defeat; Washington's survival; the Albany Congress and Franklin's Plan of Union. | Teacher narrative; *Heroes of the Revolution* (Adler); Animated Hero Classics — George Washington |

| **Day 4** | **★ Formative Quiz 3** (Lesson 4). | Quiz U1-Q3 (Form IA or IIA) |

## WEEK 9
**Unit Summative Week**

| Day | Activity | Resources |
|-----|----------|-----------|
| **Day 1** | **Review Day.** Work through the Unit Test Review Sheet together. Students self-quiz using the review sheet. Family discussion: *What was the most surprising thing you learned? What story do you most want to remember?* | Review Sheet U1-RS |
| **Day 2** | **★ Final Unit Test** — Lessons 3–4. Form IA or IIA. | Test U1-T2 |

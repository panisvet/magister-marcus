# *The New World Made Ready* — Unit 1
## Weekly Pacing Schedule
### The British Colonies of North America, 1492–1763
**4 days/week · 9 weeks · ~36 lesson sessions**

---

## How to Use This Schedule

- **Day 1** is geography, context-setting, or map work.
- **Day 2** is the core history narrative — the "story day." This is the heart of the week. Let it run long if discussion is rich.
- **Day 3** brings in a primary source, biography, living book chapter, art study, or video.
- **Day 4** is student written work: narration, copywork, notebooking page, or quiz.

**Sessions run 45–60 minutes.** Written work days are typically 30–45 minutes.

**Read-alouds** run alongside lessons as a separate daily reading time (not counted in the 4 lesson days). Aim for 2–3 chapters of the current read-aloud per week.

---

## WEEK 1
**Lesson 1 — The Land and Its Waters**
*Focus: Topographic geography of North America, east to west*

| Day | Activity | Resources |
|-----|----------|-----------|
| **Day 1** | **Intro: What Is a Map?** Read aloud *Maps and Globes* (Knowlton). Introduce map vs. globe; practice cardinal directions. Students begin their blank North America outline maps. | *Maps and Globes*; printed blank North America outline map |
| **Day 2** | **Geography Narrative: East to West.** Teacher-narrated journey: Atlantic Ocean → Appalachians → Great Plains → Rockies → Pacific. Students add features to their maps as you narrate. | Blank outline map; imagery of key landforms |
| **Day 3** | **Geography Narrative: Rivers, Lakes, and the Gulf.** Mississippi River, Great Lakes, Gulf of Mexico, Bering Strait. Discussion: *Why do rivers matter to settlers? Where would you want to live?* | Reference maps; David Rumsey collection image (printed) |
| **Day 4** | **Map Narration + Notebooking.** Students label their maps from memory using a word bank. *Form IA:* Oral narration: "Tell me the names and locations of three important waterways." *Form IIA:* Written narration (1 paragraph): "Describe the major landforms of North America from east to west." | Blank outline map; student notebooks |

**Read-Aloud This Week:** Begin *The Landing of the Pilgrims* (Daugherty), Ch. 1–2 — LibriVox audio or print

## WEEK 2
**Lesson 1 — The Peoples of America**
*Focus: Native peoples of North America before European contact*

| Day | Activity | Resources |
|-----|----------|-----------|
| **Day 1** | **Native Civilizations: An Overview.** Maya, Aztec, Inca — where they were, how they lived. Show photographs of ruins. Return to the North America map and place major eastern tribes. | Images from LOC or Britannica Kids; student map |
| **Day 2** | **Story Day: Columbus's Crew Sights Land.** Teacher narrates the scene: October 12, 1492. The long weeks at sea, the near-mutiny, the birds, the cry — *"¡Tierra!"* | Teacher narrative notes (Lesson 1 plan) |
| **Day 3** | **Living History Comparison: Native Life and English Life.** What was daily life like for a Wampanoag child? For an English child in 1600? Students listen and discuss. View 1–2 Britannica Kids articles or Colonial Williamsburg video. | Britannica Kids; CW video "Native People of Virginia" |
| **Day 4** | **Notebooking Page 1 — The Land of America.** Students illustrate a landform or native settlement and write 1–3 sentences describing what they drew. *Form IA:* 1–2 sentences. *Form IIA:* 3–4 sentences including what made this place important. | Notebooking Page U1-NB-1 |

**Read-Aloud This Week:** *The Landing of the Pilgrims*, Ch. 3–4

## WEEK 3
**Lesson 2 — Columbus and the Age of Exploration**
*Focus: Columbus; other explorers; why Europeans came west*

| Day | Activity | Resources |
|-----|----------|-----------|
| **Day 1** | **Context: Why Did Europeans Start Exploring?** Brief lesson on Muslim trade routes, Portuguese coast work, Spanish monarchy, new maritime technology. Map: Columbus's four voyages. | *Exploration & Conquest* (Maestro), Ch. 1–2; voyage map from LOC |
| **Day 2** | **Story Day: Columbus.** Full narrative of the first voyage, landfall on San Salvador, meeting the Taíno. What Columbus thought he had found. His later failures. *Discussion: Was Columbus a success or a failure?* | *Aboard the Santa Maria*; teacher narrative notes |
| **Day 3** | **Primary Source: Columbus's Letter (Excerpts).** Teacher reads paraphrased excerpts of Columbus's letter to Ferdinand and Isabella describing the New World. Students listen and respond orally. | Excerpts (teacher-prepared from public domain text); Animated Hero Classics — Columbus (video, 30 min.) |
| **Day 4** | **Narration Day — Columbus.** *Form IA:* Oral narration: "Tell back the story of Columbus — who he was, what he believed, what he found, and what happened to him in the end." *Form IIA:* Written paragraph narration (5–8 sentences) on the same prompt. | Student notebooks |

**Read-Aloud:** *Exploration & Conquest* (Maestro), Ch. 3–5 (or continue *Pilgrims*)

## WEEK 4
**Lesson 2 — Roanoke, Jamestown, Plymouth**
*Focus: First English settlements; self-government begins*

| Day | Activity | Resources |
|-----|----------|-----------|
| **Day 1** | **Roanoke: The Lost Colony.** Story of England's first attempt. The mystery. *What might have happened? Students speculate.* | *Roanoke* (Yolen), read aloud; map of Roanoke Island |
| **Day 2** | **Story Day: Jamestown.** Virginia Company, John Smith, the Starving Time, Pocahontas, tobacco, John Rolfe. *Discussion: What saved Jamestown?* | *James Towne* (Sewall), Ch. 1–3; teacher narrative notes |
| **Day 3** | **Story Day: Plymouth and the Mayflower Compact.** ★ The voyage, the first winter, Massasoit and the Wampanoag, the First Thanksgiving. Primary source: Winslow's letter (1 paragraph). Mayflower Compact text — read together and discuss. ⚖️ | *The Thanksgiving Story* (Dalgliesh); Mayflower Compact full text (avalon.law.yale.edu); Winslow excerpt |
| **Day 4** | **★ Formative Quiz 1** (Lessons 1–2). | Quiz U1-Q1 (Form IA or IIA) |

> **★ Midunit Test** may be administered at end of Week 4 or beginning of Week 5.

**Read-Aloud:** *The Landing of the Pilgrims* (Daugherty), Ch. 5–8 (complete book)

## WEEK 5
**Lesson 3 — The Colonies in Profile, Part I**
*Focus: New England and Middle colonies; founding stories; religious freedom*

| Day | Activity | Resources |
|-----|----------|-----------|
| **Day 1** | **Map Day: The 13 Colonies.** Label outline maps of the 13 colonies. Color-code the three regions: New England (blue), Middle (green), Southern (yellow). Discuss climate and geography differences. | 13 Colonies outline map |
| **Day 2** | **Story Day: New England Colonies.** Massachusetts Bay, Connecticut, Rhode Island, New Hampshire. Roger Williams and religious toleration. Winthrop's "city upon a hill." ⚖️ | Teacher narrative; *If You Lived in Colonial Times*, Ch. 1–3 |
| **Day 3** | **Story Day: Middle Colonies.** New York, New Jersey, Pennsylvania, Delaware. William Penn and the Quakers. Lord Baltimore and Maryland. Philadelphia. Art study: *William Penn's Treaty with the Indians* (Benjamin West, 1771). | Teacher narrative; *If You Lived in Colonial Times*, Ch. 4–5 |
| **Day 4** | **Notebooking Page 2 — My Colony.** Students choose one colony, illustrate a scene from its founding or daily life, and narrate in writing. *Form IA:* 2–3 sentences: who founded it and why. *Form IIA:* 4–6 sentences: founding story, key person, what made this colony distinctive. | Notebooking Page U1-NB-2 |

**Copywork this week:** *"We must consider that we shall be as a city upon a hill. The eyes of all people are upon us."* — John Winthrop (Form IA: copy; Form IIA: dictation)

**Read-Aloud:** *If You Lived in Colonial Times* (McGovern) — 2–3 sections per sitting

## WEEK 6
**Lesson 3 — The Colonies in Profile, Part II**
*Focus: Southern colonies; colonial life; slavery and the Triangle Trade*

| Day | Activity | Resources |
|-----|----------|-----------|
| **Day 1** | **Story Day: Southern Colonies.** Virginia, North/South Carolina, Georgia, Maryland. James Oglethorpe and Georgia. Cash crops: tobacco, rice, indigo. | Teacher narrative; *If You Lived in Colonial Times* (reference) |
| **Day 2** | **Story Day: Colonial Life.** What did a typical colonist's day look like? Food, clothing, shelter, schools, trades, church. *Form IA:* picture one family's day. *Form IIA:* compare daily life across colonial regions. | *If You Lived in Colonial Times* (reference); Colonial Williamsburg video resource |
| **Day 3** | **Hard History: Slavery and the Middle Passage.** The origins of African slavery in the New World, the Triangle Trade, the Middle Passage, the first Africans at Jamestown. Stories of enslaved people and of those who won their freedom. ⚖️ | Britannica Kids "Transatlantic Slave Trade"; teacher narrative notes |
| **Day 4** | **★ Formative Quiz 2** (Lesson 3). | Quiz U1-Q2 (Form IA or IIA) |

**Read-Aloud:** *The New Americans* (Maestro, B.) OR *Three Young Pilgrims* (Harness)

## WEEK 7
**Lesson 4 — Major Events, Part I**
*Focus: English political heritage; colonial self-government; Great Awakening*

| Day | Activity | Resources |
|-----|----------|-----------|
| **Day 1** | **Political Background: Magna Carta → Glorious Revolution.** The English tradition of limiting royal power. What colonists knew and believed about rights. ⚖️ | British Library Magna Carta resources; teacher narrative |
| **Day 2** | **Story Day: Self-Government in the Colonies.** Colonial assemblies (Virginia House of Burgesses). Representation. "Salutary neglect" — why England's inattention was actually good for the colonists. ⚖️ | Teacher narrative notes; Lesson 4 plan |
| **Day 3** | **The Great Awakening.** What it was, why it mattered. Jonathan Edwards, George Whitefield. How it gave colonists something in common across all 13 separate colonies. | Teacher narrative; short video clip (YouTube: "Great Awakening elementary") |
| **Day 4** | **Biography Study + Copywork: Young George Washington.** Boyhood, Rules of Civility, Virginia militia. Students copy one Rule of Civility and write 1–2 sentences about what it means and why Washington would have valued it. | *Meet George Washington* (Heilbroner); Washington's Rules of Civility (founders.archives.gov) |

**Copywork this week:** One Rule of Civility of each student's choice. (Form IA: copy by hand; Form IIA: dictation.)

**Read-Aloud:** *Ben Franklin of Old Philadelphia* (Cousins), Ch. 1–4

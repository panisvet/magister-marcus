**Short Answer / Essay — Accept responses that include:**

*Q19–21 (Form IA) / Q20–23 (Form IIA):*
- Colonial assemblies taught colonists to make their own laws; prepared them for independence
- Great Awakening created a shared religious experience across colony lines; colonists began to feel like one people
- Treaty of Paris: Britain gained all French territory east of Mississippi; France lost Canada; Spain gained territory west of Mississippi
- Glorious Revolution / English Bill of Rights: limited royal power; gave Parliament rights; colonists believed they inherited these rights as Englishmen

*Stories for the Heart (Form IA Q17–18):*
- Middle Passage: enslaved Africans chained below decks; little food or water; sickness and death widespread; brutal and dehumanizing
- Fort Duquesne: Braddock led British regulars in close formation; ambushed by French and Native warriors; Braddock killed; Washington organized the retreat; two horses shot under him; coat pierced by bullets; survived

*Essay Q24 (Form IIA — Benjamin Franklin):*
Strong responses will note: Franklin was self-made (no noble birth); rose by his own intelligence and industry; inventor (bifocals, lightning rod, stove); founded institutions (library, fire company, hospital); wrote *Poor Richard's Almanack*; proposed the Albany Plan of Union showing colonists could cooperate; all of this reflected American values of self-improvement, practical ingenuity, and civic responsibility

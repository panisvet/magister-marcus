## I. Spine & Teacher Reference

### ★ Core Teacher Text

**McClay, Wilfred M.** *Land of Hope: An Invitation to the Great American Story.* Encounter Books, 2019. 📚
> The best single-volume narrative history of America for adults teaching American history. Beautifully written, honest, and patriotic without being naive. Read the relevant chapters (Preface through Ch. 3) before teaching each lesson. *Essential.*

**McClay, Wilfred M.** *Land of Hope Young Reader's Edition, Volume 1.* Encounter Books, 2023. 📚 🔁
> Adapted for upper-elementary and middle school readers. Can be assigned as a student spine for Form IIA students (strong readers, grades 5–6). Pages xiii–xv and 1–36 cover Unit 1 content.

**Guerber, H.A.** *The Story of the Thirteen Colonies and the Great Republic.* 1898. 🆓
> Classic narrative history written for children. Dated language but excellent storytelling. Pages 11–67 are directly relevant to Unit 1.
> - **Project Gutenberg:** [gutenberg.org/ebooks/19721](https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/19721)
> - **LibriVox audiobook:** [librivox.org](https://librivox.org) — search "Story of Thirteen Colonies Guerber"

## II. Student Trade Books (Living Books)

These are the narrative, story-driven books that bring history alive. Assign one chapter or book per week as a read-aloud or independent read.

### Geography & Early Exploration
**Knowlton, Jack.** *Maps and Globes.* HarperCollins, 1985. 📚 🔁
> Simple, beautifully illustrated introduction to maps for younger elementary students. Read aloud in Week 1.

**Maestro, Betsy.** *Exploration & Conquest: The Americas After Columbus, 1500–1620.* HarperCollins, 1994. 📚 🔁
> Excellent narrative overview of the age of exploration through the settlement period. Lively illustrations. Assign during Lesson 2 weeks.

**Mikoley, Kate.** *Aboard the Santa Maria.* Gareth Stevens, 2017. 📚
> Narrative account of Columbus's first voyage, written for ages 8–12.

### Jamestown
**Sewall, Marcia.** *James Towne: Struggle for Survival.* Atheneum, 2001. 📚 🔁
> First-person voice narrative of Jamestown's founding. Striking illustrations. Read aloud during Lesson 2, Jamestown section.

**Yolen, Jane.** *Roanoke: The Lost Colony.* Simon & Schuster, 1998. 📚 🔁
> Beautifully told account of the Lost Colony mystery. Excellent read-aloud.

### Plymouth and the Pilgrims
**Daugherty, James.** *The Landing of the Pilgrims.* Random House, 1950. 📚 🔁 ★ Core
> Landmark children's history. Narrative retelling drawn from Bradford's *Of Plymouth Plantation*. Assign as a chapter-a-week read-aloud during Lessons 2–3. One of the finest children's history books ever written. **ThriftBooks: from $5.39.** LibriVox audio also available free if preferred.

**Dalgliesh, Alice.** *The Thanksgiving Story.* Scribner, 1954. 📚 🔁
> Caldecott Honor book. Warm, accessible account of the Pilgrims' first year. Read aloud in one sitting.

**McGovern, Ann.** *The Pilgrims' First Thanksgiving.* Scholastic, 1993. 📚 🔁
> Simple and direct. Good for younger students (Form IA).

### Colonial Life
**McGovern, Ann.** *If You Lived in Colonial Times.* Scholastic, 1992. 📚 🔁 ★ Core
> Q&A format covering daily life: food, clothing, school, work, punishment. Endlessly useful as a reference during Lesson 3.

**Brenner, Barbara.** *If You Lived in Williamsburg in Colonial Days.* Scholastic, 2000. 📚 🔁
> Companion to the above. Focus on Virginia colonial life.

**Maestro, Betsy.** *The New Americans: Colonial Times, 1620–1689.* HarperCollins, 1998. 📚 🔁
> Overview of immigration, settlement patterns, and diverse colonial communities.

**Harness, Cheryl.** *Three Young Pilgrims.* Simon & Schuster, 1992. 📚 🔁
> Child's-eye view of the Mayflower voyage and first year at Plymouth. Strong illustrations.

### Biography
*(See Section VI — Biography Studies — for full biography resources.)*

**Heilbroner, Joan.** *Meet George Washington.* Random House, 1964. 📚 🔁
> Step Into Reading biography. Accessible for grades 3–4.

**Cousins, Margaret.** *Ben Franklin of Old Philadelphia.* Random House, 1952. 📚 🔁
> Classic Landmark series biography. Excellent for grades 4–6.

**Adler, David A.** *Heroes of the Revolution.* Holiday House, 2003. 📚 🔁
> Profiles of key figures of the revolutionary era, useful for bridging into Unit 2.

## III. Primary Sources

All primary sources listed here are free and in the public domain.

### ★ Mayflower Compact (1620)
**Full text:** [avalon.law.yale.edu/17th_century/mayflower.asp](https://avalon.law.yale.edu/17th_century/mayflower.asp) 🆓
> One page. Read aloud and discuss in Lesson 2. Key civics document. ⚖️

### ★ William Bradford, *Of Plymouth Plantation*
**Excerpts (Internet Archive):** [archive.org/details/ofplymouthplanta00brad](https://archive.org/details/ofplymouthplanta00brad) 🆓
> Use excerpts on the Mayflower voyage, the first winter, and the First Thanksgiving. Bradford's own words about giving thanks to God are natural Providence-framing moments.

### ★ Edward Winslow's Account of the First Thanksgiving (1621)
**Full text:** Available at [plimoth.org](https://plimoth.org) and [historyofmassachusetts.org](https://historyofmassachusetts.org) 🆓
> A single paragraph — the entire primary source account of the First Thanksgiving. Read aloud. Students will be surprised at how brief it is.

### ★ John Winthrop, "A Modell of Christian Charity" (1630) — Excerpt
**Full text:** [winthropsociety.com/doc_charity.php](http://winthropsociety.com/doc_charity.php) 🆓
> Use only the "city upon a hill" passage. One paragraph. Powerful Providence connection.

### George Washington, "Rules of Civility & Decent Behaviour in Company and Conversation"
**Full list (110 rules):** [founders.archives.gov](https://founders.archives.gov) 🆓
> Assign 2–3 rules per week during Lesson 4. Students copy one rule as copywork and illustrate it. See Lesson 4 plan for suggested rules.

### Benjamin Franklin, *The Autobiography of Benjamin Franklin*
**Project Gutenberg:** [gutenberg.org/ebooks/20203](https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/20203) 🆓
**LibriVox:** [librivox.org](https://librivox.org) — search "Autobiography Franklin" 🆓
> Excerpts from Part I (childhood and early career) for Lesson 4. Assign 2–3 pages at a time for Form IIA students.

### Magna Carta — Plain English Summary
**British Library resource:** [bl.uk/magna-carta](https://www.bl.uk/magna-carta) 🆓
> Use the British Library's free educational summaries. Background for Lesson 4 civics discussion.

## VI. Geography Resources

### Maps (Public Domain / Free)

**Perry-Castañeda Library Map Collection**
[lib.utexas.edu/maps/united_states.html](https://lib.utexas.edu/maps/united_states.html) 🆓
> Blank outline maps of the U.S. and regions. Print the "Original 13 Colonies" outline map for student use.

**National Park Service — Historical Maps**
[nps.gov/subjects/nationalregister/maps.htm](https://nps.gov/subjects/nationalregister/maps.htm) 🆓
> Colonial-era maps and modern historical reference maps.

**David Rumsey Map Collection**
[davidrumsey.com](https://davidrumsey.com) 🆓
> Search "13 colonies 1763" or "colonial America 1750." High-resolution originals, downloadable free for education.

**Library of Congress Map Division**
[loc.gov/maps/collections](https://loc.gov/maps/collections) 🆓
> Search "French and Indian War map" and "colonial North America." Free high-resolution downloads.

### Generating Clean Student Maps
For lesson-ready student outline maps (blank for labeling), use an AI image tool (Grok recommended) with prompts such as:
- *"Clean black and white outline map of the thirteen original British colonies of North America, labeled with colony names, no shading, suitable for student printing and coloring"*
- *"Simple outline map of North America showing major geographic features: Appalachian Mountains, Mississippi River, Great Lakes, Rocky Mountains, labeled, black and white line art"*

Review generated maps against a reference before printing. The Perry-Castañeda blank maps are the most reliable fallback if AI-generated maps have errors.

### Geography Textbook Reference
**Core Knowledge Foundation — *The Geography of the United States: Teacher Guide*** 📚
> Referenced in Hillsdale's own lesson planning. Strong topographic and regional geography coverage.

## VII. Art & Visual Culture

These artists and works connect to Unit 1 content and are excellent notebooking illustration reference and for developing visual literacy.

### Historical Painters & Works

**John Gadsby Chapman** — *Baptism of Pocahontas* (1840) 🆓
> U.S. Capitol Rotunda painting. Free high-resolution image at [aoc.gov](https://aoc.gov). Use during Lesson 2.

**John Trumbull** — Colonial-era portraits (Washington, Franklin, etc.) 🆓
> Yale University Art Gallery has free digital access to Trumbull's complete works.
> [artgallery.yale.edu](https://artgallery.yale.edu)

**Benjamin West** — *William Penn's Treaty with the Indians* (1771) 🆓
> Pennsylvania Academy of Fine Arts. Free high-resolution image widely available. Use during Lesson 3.

**Emanuel Leutze** — *Washington Crossing the Delaware* (1851) 🆓
> Metropolitan Museum of Art, free digital access. [metmuseum.org](https://metmuseum.org/art/collection) (Search title.)
> Technically Unit 2, but powerful for introducing Washington.

**Howard Pyle** — Colonial-era illustrations 🆓
> Pyle illustrated many colonial history books; his work is public domain. Search "Howard Pyle colonial America" at [artcyclopedia.com](http://www.artcyclopedia.com).

**Norman Rockwell** — *The Mayflower Compact* (1977), *First Thanksgiving* illustrations 🆓
> Saturday Evening Post covers; widely available online. Warm, approachable for students.

### Maps as Art
The ornate colonial-era maps from the David Rumsey collection double as art study. Print a decorative colonial map (e.g., Herman Moll's *A Map of the British Empire in America*, 1715) and spend 10 minutes observing it: What do students notice? What is labeled? What is missing?

## IX. Field Trip & Living History Connections

If geography permits, these living history sites are directly relevant to Unit 1:

- **Plimoth Patuxent (Plymouth, MA)** — Living history museum; Wampanoag homesite and Mayflower replica
- **Colonial Williamsburg (Williamsburg, VA)** — Finest living history museum in the U.S. for colonial period
- **Jamestown Settlement (Williamsburg, VA)** — Adjacent to Colonial Williamsburg; replica ships and fort
- **Historic St. Mary's City (St. Mary's City, MD)** — Maryland colonial history
- **Fort Ticonderoga (Ticonderoga, NY)** — French and Indian War site

Virtual alternatives for all of the above are freely available online.

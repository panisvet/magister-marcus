# Unit 1, Lesson 2 — Exploration and Settlement
## Teacher Answer Key — Narration Prompts

**Teacher use only. Do not distribute to students.**

This key lists the content checkpoints for each narration prompt in the Lesson 2 plan. A strong narration need not hit every point — use this list to listen for understanding, not to grade like a test. Consistent gaps across multiple narrations signal a need for more reading, not more questioning.

---

## Oral Narration Prompts

---

### Prompt: "Tell me the story of Columbus — who he was, what he believed about the world, what he found, and what happened to him in the end."
*(Classes 1–2)*

**Strong narration includes:**
- Columbus was a sailor from Genoa (Italy)
- He believed the world was smaller than others thought — he could reach Asia by sailing west
- Ferdinand and Isabella of Spain funded his voyage
- He sailed in 1492 on three ships: *Niña*, *Pinta*, *Santa María*
- He landed on San Salvador (a small island in the Caribbean/Bahamas)
- He called the people he met "Indians" — he thought he'd reached the Indies
- He made four voyages total
- He died never knowing he had discovered a new half of the world
- His voyages connected the Old World and New World for the first time

**Acceptable for Form IA:** Columbus sailed west, found land, thought it was Asia, made multiple trips, died not knowing what he'd found.

**Expected for Form IIA:** All major beats above; Columbian Exchange mentioned or implied; honest assessment of whether he succeeded (found land, changed history — but never found Asia, died a failure in his own eyes).

---

### Prompt: "Tell me the story of the 'Starving Time' at Jamestown. What happened? What saved the colony?"
*(Class 4)*

**Strong narration includes:**
- Jamestown founded 1607 by the Virginia Company for profit (gold)
- 500 settlers by winter of 1609–1610; most died
- They ate horses, dogs, rats, leather — desperate conditions
- John Smith's leadership had helped earlier, but he was gone by the Starving Time
- Lord De La Warr arrived in 1610 with new settlers and supplies — colony survived
- What saved Jamestown long-term: John Rolfe's tobacco as a cash crop; House of Burgesses (1619, first elected assembly); arrival of women and families

**Acceptable for Form IA:** Most settlers died; Lord De La Warr saved the colony; tobacco made it successful.

**Expected for Form IIA:** Full arc from founding through Starving Time to recovery; specific causes of failure and success named; House of Burgesses identified as self-government.

---

### Prompt: "Tell me the story of the Mayflower voyage and the first winter at Plymouth. What did the Pilgrims hope for? What did they find?"
*(Class 6)*

**Strong narration includes:**
- Pilgrims were Separatists — wanted to separate from the Church of England
- Left from England in September 1620 on the *Mayflower*; 102 passengers, 66 days at sea
- Storms, cramped conditions, one death at sea, one birth
- Landed at Plymouth in November 1620 — outside their original patent
- Before going ashore, 41 men signed the Mayflower Compact
- First winter: half the settlers died of cold and disease
- Spring: Squanto came and taught them to plant corn; Massasoit made a treaty
- Fall 1621: First Thanksgiving — Massasoit and 90 Wampanoag came; three days of celebration

**Acceptable for Form IA:** Left England for religious freedom; hard voyage; terrible first winter; Squanto and Massasoit helped; First Thanksgiving.

**Expected for Form IIA:** Separatist identity; Mayflower Compact as self-government; specific details of the winter (deaths, hardship); Squanto's remarkable backstory (enslaved, taken to England, returned); Massasoit's treaty.

---

### Prompt: "Tell me about one of these: Roanoke, Jamestown, Plymouth, or Massachusetts Bay — what was it, who settled it, and why does it matter?"
*(Class 8 — student's choice)*

**Content checkpoints by settlement:**

**Roanoke:**
- England's first attempt at a permanent settlement, 1580s
- Funded by Sir Walter Raleigh; off the coast of present-day North Carolina
- The entire colony disappeared — no one knows what happened
- The word "CROATOAN" was carved on a post
- Matters because: it showed England the difficulty of settlement; the mystery has never been solved

**Jamestown:**
- First permanent English settlement in America, 1607
- Founded by Virginia Company for profit; built on a swampy, disease-ridden site
- Suffered starvation, disease, conflict with Powhatan Confederacy
- Saved by John Smith's discipline, De La Warr's arrival, Rolfe's tobacco
- First elected assembly (House of Burgesses, 1619) — first self-government in America
- Matters because: proved England could permanently settle North America; gave birth to self-government

**Plymouth:**
- Founded by Pilgrims (Separatists) in 1620; sought religious freedom
- Mayflower Compact = first act of self-government in New England
- Massasoit's alliance essential to survival
- First Thanksgiving 1621
- Matters because: religious freedom as a founding motive; self-government; Thanksgiving tradition

**Massachusetts Bay:**
- Founded by Puritans in 1630; John Winthrop led 700 settlers in 11 ships
- Winthrop's "city upon a hill" — a visible example of godly community
- Grew rapidly; became the largest New England colony
- Matters because: Puritan work ethic and self-governance shaped New England character; "city upon a hill" idea has echoed through American history

## Written Narration Prompts — Form IA

---

### Prompt: "Write about what Columbus found when he landed in the New World."

**Strong narration includes:**
- He landed on a small island (San Salvador / Caribbean)
- He met the Taíno people (or "Indians" — the name he gave them)
- He thought he had reached Asia / the East Indies
- He claimed the land for Spain
- Complete sentences

**Watch for:** Students who just say "he found land." Push for: what kind of land, who was there, what did he think it was?

---

### Prompt: "Write 2–3 sentences about why the Pilgrims came to America and what they hoped to build."

**Strong narration includes:**
- They wanted religious freedom (to worship without government interference)
- They were Separatists — they had separated from the Church of England
- They hoped to build a community where they could live and worship freely
- Complete sentences

---

### Prompt: "Write about what the Mayflower Compact promised."

**Strong narration includes:**
- The Pilgrims agreed to govern themselves
- They would make fair laws for the good of everyone
- They signed it before going ashore at Plymouth
- It was an early example of self-government

**Watch for:** Students who describe it as a religious document. It is a civil agreement — about government, not worship.

## Written Narration Prompts — Form IIA

---

### Prompt: "Write a paragraph about Columbus's voyages — his theory, what he found, and why his voyages changed history."

**Strong narration includes:**
- His theory: world smaller than believed; could reach Asia by sailing west
- Common misconception corrected: people didn't think the world was flat — Columbus thought it was smaller
- Ferdinand and Isabella funded the voyage; 1492, three ships
- Found the Caribbean; called inhabitants Indians
- Made four voyages; gradual decline in success
- Columbian Exchange: plants, animals, diseases moved between hemispheres
- Smallpox devastating to Native Americans who had no immunity
- Changed history: connected Old World and New World for the first time; started European civilization's influence on the Western Hemisphere
- 5–8 sentences

---

### Prompt: "Write a paragraph about the founding of Jamestown — who funded it, what they suffered, and what saved it."

**Strong narration includes:**
- Virginia Company funded it — profit motive (looking for gold)
- 1607; built on a swampy site; disease, conflict with Powhatan Confederacy
- John Smith's discipline helped early survival
- Starving Time 1609–1610: catastrophic death toll (eating horses, leather)
- Saved by: De La Warr's arrival; Rolfe's tobacco as cash crop; House of Burgesses 1619
- House of Burgesses = first elected assembly = self-government
- 5–8 sentences

---

### Prompt: "Write a paragraph about the Mayflower Compact — what it was, who signed it, and why it was an early example of self-government."

**Strong narration includes:**
- Signed before going ashore at Plymouth, November 1620
- 41 men signed it
- They agreed to combine into a "civil Body Politick" — to govern themselves
- Would make just and equal laws for the common good
- Signed because they had landed outside their original patent — no legal authority governed them
- This is self-government: ordinary people agreeing together to make their own laws
- Connects to the larger tradition leading to the Constitution
- 5–8 sentences

---

### Prompt: "Write a paragraph about Winthrop's 'city upon a hill' — what it meant and why it has remained important in American history."

**Strong narration includes:**
- John Winthrop led Puritans to Massachusetts Bay in 1630
- Preached the sermon on the ship *Arbella* before landing
- "City upon a hill": their community would be visible — a model for the world
- If they succeeded in building a just, godly community, others would see it; if they failed, others would see that too
- It was an expression of purpose, responsibility, and accountability before God and the world
- This phrase has been quoted by American leaders ever since — the idea that America has a special purpose or responsibility
- 5–8 sentences

---

## "To Know by Heart" Recitation Check

Both forms should be able to recite from memory by end of lesson:

> *"We must consider that we shall be as a city upon a hill. The eyes of all people are upon us."* — John Winthrop

**What to listen for:** Accurate wording; student can identify the speaker (Winthrop) and the occasion (Massachusetts Bay, 1630, before landing).

## Common Narration Problems and Responses

**Student confuses Pilgrims and Puritans:**
Return to Class 7 material. Key distinction: Pilgrims = Separatists, left the Church of England entirely, sailed 1620, Plymouth. Puritans = reformers within the Church, sailed 1630, Massachusetts Bay. Both sought religious freedom but for different reasons and in different ways.

**Student blurs the four settlements together:**
Do not drill with questions. Assign one more read-aloud session focused on a single settlement. Ask for a narration about that one settlement only before broadening.

**Student narrates Columbus correctly but has no sense of the Columbian Exchange:**
This is normal at this stage — it is a complex concept. Return to it briefly in Lesson 3 when you discuss how tobacco, corn, and other crops shaped colonial life.

**Student gives the Mayflower Compact a religious rather than civic interpretation:**
Clarify: the Compact was about government, not worship. They were agreeing to make laws and choose leaders — not writing a religious creed. Both purposes (godly community + self-government) were present in their minds, but the Compact itself is a civil document.

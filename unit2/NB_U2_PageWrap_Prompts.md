## OPTION A — The Founding Garden
*Botanical border with Atlantic seaboard plants and colonial fauna — warm palette, visual continuity with Unit 1*

### Design Intent
Same living botanical border structure as Unit 1. The New World plants shift to the cultivated and agricultural plants of the colonial Atlantic seaboard — wheat, apple blossom, clover, wild rose added alongside the tobacco, grape, maize, and fern that carry over. Fauna is nearly identical to Unit 1, providing strong series continuity. The 8 historical insets do the thematic work of signaling the new unit. Warm ochre/sienna/moss green palette maintained.

### Nano Banana Prompt — Option A

```

BACKGROUND: Full-bleed antique cream parchment with aged texture and soft

with the density and integration of an illuminated manuscript border.
```

## OPTION B — The Broadside Border
*Oak, laurel, and classical ornament border — cooler formal palette, signals new era*

### Design Intent
Replaces New World botanicals with a period-appropriate political and natural symbolism: oak branches (liberty/strength), laurel (classical republic), ivy, and the visual language of 18th-century printed broadsides and pamphlets. Palette shifts to cooler, more formal tones — navy, pewter, aged gold, deep red — appropriate for the era of founding documents. Only two fauna: bald eagle (emerging national symbol) and rattlesnake (*Don't Tread On Me*). The result should feel like a beautifully illuminated version of a 1776 broadside — still handmade and warm, but more formal and architectural than Unit 1.

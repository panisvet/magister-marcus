## Scope Finding (read first)

The crosscheck file contains **no American history topics**. Its four sections are Ancient Egypt (29), Ancient Greece & Rome (29), Historical Thinking (7), and Medieval Times (25). Topic-by-topic content matching against a 1492–1877 curriculum is therefore not applicable — and there is **no content conflict**: the taxonomy's chronology ends at Printing Press & Renaissance (ages 9–11), exactly where Unit 1 Lesson 2 picks up ("growing curiosity in European culture after the Renaissance" ✓).

The meaningful validation axes are:

1. The **7 Historical Thinking skills** (cross-cutting, ages 6–11 — squarely the curriculum's grade 3–6 / ages 8–12 band)
2. **Civics prerequisite topics** the taxonomy expects before republic/representation content (Athenian Democracy 7–9, Roman Republic and Empire 9–11, Magna Carta 9–11)
3. **Chronology/timeline** practice

## Verdict

**Validated with 3 gaps.** Content is fully compatible with the crosscheck taxonomy (no overlaps, no contradictions, correct chronological handoff). Contextualization ("people in their own time") and Magna Carta coverage are exemplary. The curriculum under-serves the taxonomy's source-skills spine: historical-thinking vocabulary, sourcing habit, and corroboration.

## Recommended Fixes (all CM-compatible, no worksheets)

1. **Add historical-thinking terms to unit vocabulary lists** — *primary source / secondary source, evidence, bias, corroborate, interpretation* — folded into existing Terms sections (fixes the hard-prereq gap).
2. **One-line sourcing habit** before each primary source read-aloud: teacher asks *who wrote this, when, and why* orally. Zero page-count cost.
3. **Reframe Boston Massacre (U2_L1) as a two-accounts narration:** "Tell back the event as Revere's engraving shows it; then tell it back as the trial evidence showed it. Why do they differ?" — covers Different Accounts + Corroboration in one existing lesson.
4. **Source the Ferris painting (U1_L2):** add one question — "This was painted in 1912, almost 300 years later. Is it evidence of 1621, or of what Americans in 1912 imagined?" — covers Questioning Sources + Evidence vs. Interpretation.
5. **One-paragraph Athens/Rome bridge in U3_L1** where "republic" is defined: Athens (citizens vote directly) vs. Rome (citizens elect representatives) vs. the American design. Satisfies the taxonomy's hard prerequisite chain for republic content.

Estimated effort: edits to 3 existing files (U1_L2, U2_L1, U3_L1) plus vocabulary-list additions per unit. No new files or books required; no budget impact.

Form IIA · Written Narration
Write five paragraphs — one for each class. Use at least 12 vocabulary words.
Fish: the first vertebrates, gills, lateral line, the ancient armored fish Buckley describes, what the backbone makes possible
Amphibians: life in two worlds, why the frog is a compromise, what Ichthyostega represents in the fossil record
Reptiles: the amniotic egg as great invention, scales and lungs, Archaeopteryx as the bridge to birds
Birds: what connects them to theropod dinosaurs, hollow bones, feathers, Audubon's method and cost
Mammals: warm-bloodedness and its cost, extended parental care and intelligence, Lobo and what his story teaches
Form IIA · Naturalist's Report
Choose one wild vertebrate you have actually observed during this unit. Write a full naturalist's report: (1) animal name and class; (2) three reasons it belongs to that class, drawn from body and behavior; (3) place, weather, date of observation; (4) what it was doing; (5) one question you still have about it.

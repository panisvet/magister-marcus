⬡ Experiment — Breathing Rate and CO₂
Part A
Breathing rate at rest: Count breaths for 1 full minute while relaxed. Record. Then exercise for 60 seconds and count immediately after. Record.
Part B
Making CO₂ visible: Breathe out slowly onto a cold glass or mirror. Fog appears. This is mostly water vapor — but exhaled air also contains about 4% CO₂ (inhaled air has only 0.04%). Ask: where did that extra CO₂ come from?
Optional
Blow through a straw into a glass of water for 30 seconds. The water becomes very slightly acidic (CO₂ dissolves to form carbonic acid). Test with pH strips if available.

⬡ Experiment — Ruler Drop Reaction Time
You need
A 30cm ruler. Two people.
Method
One person holds the ruler vertically at the top. The other places thumb and forefinger around (not touching) the 0cm end. Without warning, the holder drops the ruler. The catcher grabs it as fast as possible. Record the centimeter mark caught. Repeat 5 times. Average the results.
Variation
Try once with the catcher's eyes closed, warned by a spoken "go." Try with the dominant hand, then the non-dominant hand. Try immediately after 10 jumping jacks.

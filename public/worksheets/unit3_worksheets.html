The Five Classes of Vertebrates

  • Fish — gills, fins, scales, eggs in water
  • Amphibian — moist skin, two lives
  • Reptile — dry scales, eggs on land
  • Bird — feathers, hollow bones
  • Mammal — fur, milk, warm blood

Key Vocabulary

Vertebrate — an animal with a backbone of vertebrae.

Invertebrate — an animal without a backbone (worm, octopus).

Skeleton — the inner frame of bones that holds the body up.

How a Fish Breathes

Water enters the mouth → flows over the gills (which take in oxygen) → leaves under the operculum.

A fish breathes water; that is why a fish lives in water.

Key Vocabulary

Gill — the organ a fish uses to take oxygen from water.

Operculum — the bony cover protecting the gills.

Lateral line — a sense organ along the side that feels water-pressure changes.

Lesson
Three-part synthesis: oral narration, written reflection, connecting question.
The Unit's QuestionYou made charge cling and crackle, followed Franklin to the lightning, built a working circuit, revealed a magnet's hidden field, and found that electricity and magnetism are two faces of one force.

The question: these forces are completely invisible — you never once saw electricity or magnetism itself, only what they DID: a spark, a lit bulb, a swinging needle, a lifted paperclip. Almost everything powerful in nature acts unseen. What does it mean that the most useful forces in the world are known only by their effects, never by direct sight?
Narration

Tell the story of electricity and magnetism, from the balloon to the electromagnet.
Form IIA · Written
Narrate the unit as one connected account. Then answer the connecting question in a paragraph.

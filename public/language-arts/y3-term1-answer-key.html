Year 3 · Term 1 — Teacher Answer Key

Keep separate from the student booklet.

Week 1 · A Sentence Is One Complete Thought
a S · b NT · c S · d NT · e S · f NT (no naming part).
Week 2 · Nouns — Naming Words
a king, ship, island · b Perseus, sword, shield · c women, eye, tooth · d goddess, boy, shield · e head, Medusa, men, stone · f waves, rocks.
Week 3 · Proper Nouns &amp; Capital Letters
Britain, Romans · Arthur, England · France, Dover · King Alfred · London, York (and the first word of each sentence).
Week 4 · Verbs — Telling Words
a sang · b runs, tumbles · c listened, smiled · d shook · e carried · f woke.
Week 5 · End Marks: . ? !
a . · b ? · c ! · d ? · e . · f ! (or .)
Week 6 · Complete Thought vs. Fragment
a S · b F · c S · d F · e S · f F. (Fix a fragment by adding the missing part.)
Week 7 · Review — Narrate &amp; Mark
a nouns duke, brother; verb forgave · b storm, ship, rocks; broke · c Miranda, shore; watched · d magician, staff; raised · e sailors, island; swam.
Week 8 · Adjectives — Describing Words
a hard, clumsy · b tiny, bright, old · c merry, bold · d deep, cold, damp · e Two, frightened, narrow.
Week 9 · Strong vs. Weak Adjectives
any vivid, fitting adjective — e.g. a dewy morning; a bristling spider; a snug nest; a raging storm; a trickling stream.
Week 10 · Adverbs
a quickly (how) · b Soon (when) · c everywhere (where) · d heavily (how) · e Yesterday (when) · f outside (where).
Week 11 · Adjectives + Adverbs Together
loyal ADJ, fiercely ADV · golden ADJ, brightly ADV · swift ADJ, wildly ADV · gentle ADJ, softly ADV · tall ADJ, firmly ADV.
Week 12 · Term Review — A Describing Paragraph
Term check: complete thought vs. fragment; find a noun and a verb; capitalize a proper noun; use exact adjectives + one adverb.

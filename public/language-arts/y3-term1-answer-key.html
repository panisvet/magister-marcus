Year 3 · Term 1 — Teacher Answer Key

Keep this separate from the student booklet. Score Conventions on the child’s own pen only.

Week 1 · A Sentence Is One Complete Thought
a = whole thought · b = not (no telling part — just a naming phrase) · c = whole thought.
Watch for: scribe the narration; the child's pen is only for the copywork line.
Week 2 · Nouns — Naming Words
a = king, ship, island · b = Perseus, sword, shield · c = women, eye. (Accept "old women" discussion.)
Week 3 · Proper Nouns &amp; Capital Letters
a = The story of Britain … the Romans. · b = A boy named Arthur lived in England.
Week 4 · Verbs — Telling Words
a = sang · b = runs, tumbles · c = listened, smiled.
Week 5 · End Marks: . ? !
a = .   b = ?   c = !
Week 6 · Complete Thought vs. Fragment
a = S · b = F (no telling part) · c = S · d = F. To fix a fragment, add the missing part: "The little lamp in her hand glowed."
Week 7 · Narrate &amp; Mark
a = nouns duke, brother; verb forgave · b = nouns storm, ship, rocks; verb broke.
Week 8 · Adjectives — Describing Words
a = hard, clumsy · b = tiny, bright, old. (Accept "a/the" discussion as articles.)
Week 9 · Strong vs. Weak Adjectives
any vivid, fitting adjective — e.g., a dewy / radiant morning; a bristling / patient spider. Praise exactness, not fanciness.
Week 10 · Adverbs
a = quickly (how) · b = soon (when) · c = everywhere (where).
Week 11 · Adjectives + Adverbs Together
a = loyal (ADJ, describes soldier), fiercely (ADV, describes fought) · b = golden (ADJ), brightly (ADV).
Week 12 · A Describing Paragraph
Term skill check: Can the child (1) tell a complete thought from a fragment, (2) find a noun and a verb, (3) capitalize a proper noun, (4) use exact adjectives + one adverb? Note growth on the 4-point rubric — Conventions on his own pen only.

Week 1

A Sentence Is One Complete Thought

Read: The Heroes (Kingsley) — the opening of Perseus.  ·  Memory: Psalm 23:1.

Day 1 Read aloud; narrate (you scribe).   Day 2 Copywork below; say what a sentence is — "one complete thought that could stand alone."   Day 3 Practice.   Day 4 Game: you say groups of words; child claps if it's a whole thought.   Day 5 Copy the memory line; recite.

Copywork

Choose one short, complete sentence the child liked from today's reading — or use: "The gods gave Perseus a pair of winged sandals."

Practice — thought or not?

a. Perseus sailed across the sea.  

b. the shining winged sandals  

c. A wise goddess helped him.  

Key: a = whole thought · b = not (no telling part — just a naming phrase) · c = whole thought.
Watch for: scribe the narration; the child's pen is only for the copywork line.

Week 2

Nouns — Naming Words

Read: The Heroes — Perseus and the Gray Sisters.  ·  Memory: Psalm 23:1.

Day 1 Read; narrate.   Day 2 Copywork; "a noun names a person, place, or thing."   Day 3 Practice — underline nouns.   Day 4 Noun hunt around the room.   Day 5 Memory copywork; recite.

Copywork

"The hero found a cave beside the cold sea."

Practice — underline every noun

a. The king sent a ship to the island.

b. Perseus carried a sword and a shield.

c. The old women guarded one eye.

Key: a = king, ship, island · b = Perseus, sword, shield · c = women, eye. (Accept "old women" discussion.)

Week 3

Proper Nouns & Capital Letters

Read: An Island Story (Marshall) — an early chapter.  ·  Memory: Psalm 23:2.

Day 1 Read; narrate.   Day 2 Copywork; "a proper noun names a particular person or place, and begins with a capital."   Day 3 Practice — fix the capitals.   Day 4 List family & place names.   Day 5 Memory copywork.

Copywork

"Long ago the Romans came to Britain."

Practice — rewrite with the right capitals

a. the story of britain begins with the romans.

b. a boy named arthur lived in england.

Key: a = The story of Britain … the Romans. · b = A boy named Arthur lived in England.

Verbs — Telling Words

Read: Parables from Nature (Gatty) — one parable.  ·  Memory: Psalm 23:2.

Day 1 Read; narrate.   Day 2 Copywork; "a verb tells what someone or something does."   Day 3 Practice — circle the verbs.   Day 4 Act out verbs; child names them.   Day 5 Memory copywork.

Copywork

"The little seed sleeps, wakes, and grows toward the light."

Practice — circle every verb

a. The bird sang in the tall tree.

b. The stream runs and tumbles down the hill.

c. The child listened and smiled.

Key: a = sang · b = runs, tumbles · c = listened, smiled.

Week 5

End Marks: . ? !

Read: Pilgrim's Progress (Bunyan) — this week's ~800 words.  ·  Memory: Psalm 23:3.

Day 1 Read; narrate.   Day 2 Copywork; a telling sentence ends with a period, a question with "?", a strong feeling with "!".   Day 3 Practice.   Day 4 Read a page aloud, child names each end mark.   Day 5 Memory copywork.

Copywork

"Which way must I go? I will not turn back!"

Practice — add the right end mark

a. The pilgrim carried a heavy burden

b. Where does this narrow path lead

c. Run from the danger

Key: a = .   b = ?   c = !

Week 6

Complete Thought vs. Fragment

Read: The Princess and the Goblin (MacDonald) — ch. 1.  ·  Memory: Psalm 23:3.

Day 1 Read; narrate.   Day 2 Copywork; a fragment is a piece — it is missing its naming or its telling part.   Day 3 Practice — S (sentence) or F (fragment).   Day 4 Fix two fragments aloud.   Day 5 Memory copywork.

Copywork

"There was once a little princess who lived in a great house among the mountains."

Practice — write S or F

a. The princess ran up the stair.

b. up the long winding stair

c. A light shone under the door.

d. the little lamp in her hand

Key: a = S · b = F (no telling part) · c = S · d = F. To fix a fragment, add the missing part: "The little lamp in her hand glowed."

Week 7 · Review

Narrate & Mark

Read: Tales from Shakespeare (Lamb) — one tale (≈ half this week).  ·  Memory: Psalm 23:4.

Day 1–2 Read the tale over two days; narrate each part.   Day 3 Copywork; then in the copied sentence, underline the nouns and circle the verbs.   Day 4 Practice.   Day 5 Memory copywork; recite Psalm 23:1–4.

Copywork

Choose a favorite sentence from the tale (2–3 lines), best hand.

Practice — underline nouns, circle verbs

a. The duke forgave his brother.

b. The storm broke the ship on the rocks.

Key: a = nouns duke, brother; verb forgave · b = nouns storm, ship, rocks; verb broke.

Week 8

Adjectives — Describing Words

Read: The Princess and the Goblin — a later chapter.  ·  Memory: Psalm 23:4.

Day 1 Read; narrate.   Day 2 Copywork; "an adjective describes a noun — which one? what kind? how many?"   Day 3 Practice — underline adjectives.   Day 4 Describe an object with 3 adjectives.   Day 5 Memory copywork.

Copywork

"A silver thread led her down the dark and winding passage."

Practice — underline the adjectives

a. The goblins had hard heads and clumsy feet.

b. A tiny, bright lamp burned in the old tower.

Key: a = hard, clumsy · b = tiny, bright, old. (Accept "a/the" discussion as articles.)

Week 9

Strong vs. Weak Adjectives

Read: Parables from Nature (Gatty) — one parable.  ·  Memory: Psalm 23:5.

Day 1 Read; narrate.   Day 2 Copywork; notice how an exact adjective paints a clearer picture than a dull one.   Day 3 Practice — upgrade the adjective.   Day 4 Describe a creature from the parable.   Day 5 Memory copywork.

Copywork

"The gossamer threads glittered, wet and silver, in the morning air."

Practice — replace the weak word (say why yours is better)

a. a nice morning → a morning

b. a big spider → a spider

Key: any vivid, fitting adjective — e.g., a dewy / radiant morning; a bristling / patient spider. Praise exactness, not fanciness.

Week 10

Adverbs

Read: The Heroes (Kingsley) — Perseus and Medusa.  ·  Memory: Psalm 23:5.

Day 1 Read; narrate.   Day 2 Copywork; "an adverb tells how, when, or where — often about a verb."   Day 3 Practice — find the adverb.   Day 4 "How did he move?" game.   Day 5 Memory copywork.

Copywork

"He flew swiftly and landed softly upon the rock."

Practice — underline the adverb; say how/when/where

a. Perseus struck quickly.   ()

b. Soon the hero flew home.   ()

c. He looked everywhere.   ()

Key: a = quickly (how) · b = soon (when) · c = everywhere (where).

Week 11

Adjectives + Adverbs Together

Read: An Island Story (Marshall) — a chapter.  ·  Memory: Psalm 23:6.

Day 1 Read; narrate.   Day 2 Copywork; adjectives describe nouns, adverbs describe verbs.   Day 3 Practice — label ADJ or ADV.   Day 4 Describe a person doing something (one of each).   Day 5 Memory copywork.

Copywork

"The brave knight rode bravely into the dark wood."

Practice — write ADJ or ADV over each underlined word

a. The loyal soldier fought fiercely.

b. A golden crown gleamed brightly.

Key: a = loyal (ADJ, describes soldier), fiercely (ADV, describes fought) · b = golden (ADJ), brightly (ADV).

Week 12 · Term Review

A Describing Paragraph

Read: Blake, "The Lamb" or "The Tyger" (poem).  ·  Memory: recite Psalm 23 whole.

Day 1 Read the poem aloud twice; narrate what it pictures.   Day 2 Copywork of a couplet.   Day 3–4 Build a describing paragraph aloud (you scribe), then the child writes his best 3–4 sentences.   Day 5 Read it back with delight; recite the psalm.

Copywork

"Little Lamb, who made thee? / Dost thou know who made thee?"

Write — describe one thing so well a reader can picture it

Use at least two exact adjectives and one adverb. Say it aloud first.

Term skill check: Can the child (1) tell a complete thought from a fragment, (2) find a noun and a verb, (3) capitalize a proper noun, (4) use exact adjectives + one adverb? Note growth on the 4-point rubric — Conventions on his own pen only.

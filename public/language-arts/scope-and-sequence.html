Why two tracks

  • Year 3 (age 8, on level). Grammar as delight and naming — meet a sentence in a living book, notice how it works. This makes explicit the grammar spine already in your prep course.
  • Year 6 (age 11, grammar ≈ grade 5). A bridge: opens at a grade-5 footing (parts of speech and their functions) and climbs across the year to grade-6 / workbook rigor. Reading stays at age level (AO Y6); grammar mechanics meet him where he is and rise — no hurry. The year ends inside the Imitation & Analysis Workbook.

Accommodations (both students struggle with spelling/handwriting)

Transcription never gates thinking — the child composes and analyzes orally; you scribe. He writes only copywork, dictation, and his own imitation. Year 3: copywork only. Year 6: studied (prepared) dictation doubles as the spelling engine (study → dictate → prevent every error); dictation words become the week's spelling list. See prepared-dictation-and-scribing.md. The 4-point rubric scores Conventions on the child's own pen only, so spelling struggle never masks strong thinking.

Editorial standards (applied to every booklet)

  • Define every new tag with a mini-lesson. No abbreviation or label (S/DO/OP/PN/APP, clause types, verbals, figure names) is ever used in practice before a short student-facing lesson defines it — plain definition, the question that finds it, a bolded example, and one worked model. Lessons are taught inductively (discover, then name), and each stays in the booklet as a year-long reference.
  • No reprinting your books. Copywork/dictation is parent-selected from the book in hand, or a short verified public-domain line; practice sentences are original.
  • Scribe everything but copywork, dictation, and imitation. Conventions scored on the child's own pen only.
  • Print-ready US Letter, one self-contained HTML file per term.

Assessment (both tracks)

Mostly oral and observational; the written footprint is small by design. Daily: narration + correct identification of the week's focus. Weekly: one short marked/parsed sentence (Y3) or one analyzed sentence + clean dictation (Y6). End-of-term: a short skill check + (Y6) one analyzed passage. One 4-point rubric all year — Ideas · Organization · Conventions · Voice — with Conventions scored on the child's own pen only.

Handoff

Year 3 ends ready for the prep course's imitation term. Year 6 ends inside the Imitation & Analysis Workbook (weeks 31–36 use its real passages) — no gap between grammar course and workbook.

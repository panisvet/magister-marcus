Year 3 · Daily Strip

Today I Write

Tiny and daily — three traced words, one line, three spelling words. Two minutes, then done.

Font shown: Berner Basisschrift (a free teaching hand, single-story a and g).

1 · Trace, then write your own

cat
pig
sun

2 · Copy one sentence (best hand)

The cat sat in the sun.

3 · Spelling — say it, cover it, write it

cat  ·  pig  ·  sun   (pattern: short-vowel c-v-c)

My writing streak — color a star each day you write:

★ ★ ★ ★ ★    5 stars = choose the next read-aloud!

Why so small? He reads far above grade level; the only thing lagging is willingness to write. Keep it short, daily, and winnable — success is what melts resistance. Scribe everything else. Build the load gently across Terms 1–2 (add a second sentence in Term 2) to reach "up to speed" by the end of Term 2.

Cursive & Dictation — Every Day

New American Cursive · you write the model, he copies · then one dictation sentence

1 · Letters that join today: a   c   d

Parent: write each letter in New American Cursive on the first line (the printed guide below is only an approximation — your hand is the model). He copies to the end of the line.

a    c    d

2 · A joined word

cave

3 · Copy one cursive line (from this week's reading)

4 · Studied dictation — one sentence (in cursive)

Study the 2–3 hard words first; parent dictates slowly and prevents every error.

Writing every day this week — the goal for Term 1 is consistency. Check each day he writes:

MonTueWedThuFri
◯◯◯◯◯
Goal: writing consistently by the end of Term 1. Keep the load light (his struggle is the pen, not the mind) but daily — cursive warm-up + one copied line + one dictation sentence. The printed cursive above is a stand-in; swap in a licensed New American Cursive font later, or keep writing the models yourself.

Year 3 · Term 3 — Teacher Answer Key

Keep separate from the student booklet.

Week 25 · Compound Sentences (and / but / or)
e.g. “The wolves howled, and the night was cold.” · “…Law, and Bagheera guarded…” · “Mowgli could stay, or he could go.” · “Shere Khan roared, but the cubs did not run.”
Week 26 · The Comma with the Conjunction
…roared, but… · …rock, and… · run, or… · old, but… · slept, and…
Week 27 · Complex Sentences (because / when / if)
any sensible clause, e.g. “…because winter was near.” · “When the sun rose, the flowers opened.” · “…if it has water and light.”
Week 28 · Relative Clauses (who / which / that)
e.g. “the narrow gate that led to life” · “a pilgrim who feared nothing” · “the heavy load which he carried” · “the shining city that stood on the hill.”
Week 29 · Compound-Complex Sentences
whole sentences are the two that could stand alone; the circled part begins with When/Because.
Week 30 · Sort All Four Sentence Types
a S · b CD · c CX · d CC · e CD · f S.
Week 31 · Simile
any fitting comparison, e.g. as quiet as a mouse; as brave as a lion; like great white ships; as bright as gold; like the wind.
Week 32 · Metaphor
e.g. “The night sky is a dark blanket sewn with stars.” Accept any true metaphor (says one thing IS another).
Week 33 · Personification
e.g. “The old gate groaned as it swung.” Any human action given to the thing.
Week 34 · Studied Copywork
Talk about how the sentence is built — where it joins, where it pauses.
Week 35 · Imitating a Sentence Shape
Praise a sentence that follows the model's rhythm, not its exact words.
Week 36 · Capstone — Imitation &amp; Recitation
Ready for the imitation term when he can narrate fully, mark the parts of a sentence, and imitate a shape.

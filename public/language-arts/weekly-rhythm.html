Year 3 — Weekly Rhythm

A gentle, repeatable week. Short and warm; scribe everything except copywork, dictation, and the child’s own writing.

DayReading & NarrationLanguage workMemory / Recitation
MonRead the living book aloud; child narrates (you scribe).Introduce the week’s grammar focus inductively.Say the poem & Psalm line together.
TueRe-read a portion; quick narration.Copywork — the model line, best hand.Poem.
WedRead; narrate.Mark / find the grammar focus; discussion.Psalm.
ThuRead; narrate.Hands-on grammar game or sort.Saint of the week (Paterikon).
FriLight review reading.One sentence of imitation or description; read it back with delight.Recite poem & Psalm.
Grammar-stage lessons stay ~15–20 min. The reading spine (AmblesideOnline Year 3) carries content; language work is short and daily.

Year 6 — Weekly Rhythm

A gentle, repeatable week. Short and warm; scribe everything except copywork, dictation, and the child’s own writing.

DayReading & NarrationLanguage workMemory / Recitation
MonRead; narrate; spot the week’s grammar in the text.Teach the focus (define any new term first).Poem; Psalm line.
TueRead; narrate.Copywork (cursive); begin studied dictation (study day).Poem.
WedRead; narrate.Dictation (write day — prevent errors); begin worksheet.Psalm; Latin root of the week.
ThuRead; narrate.Analyze a real passage (parse / label / sort); finish worksheet.Saint (Prologue of Ochrid).
FriRead; narrate.Imitation of a studied sentence; read back & fix one thing.Recite poem & Psalm; review the root’s derivatives.
~25–30 min. His pen is only for copywork, one dictation sentence, and his imitation — everything analytical is oral and scribed. Dictation is prepared (study → dictate → prevent).

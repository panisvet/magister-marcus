Cursive Strip — Week 1

Grammar focus this week: The Noun at Work. The strip is the day’s pen-work — nothing here is extra.

1 · Letters that join this week: a   c   d

Parent: write the models in New American Cursive on the first line — your hand is the model; the printed guide is a stand-in. He copies to the end of the line.

a   c   d

2 · A joined word from the week’s sentence

made

3 · Copy the week’s line (best cursive)

Prometheus took some of this earth, and kneading it up with water, made man in the image of the gods.

— Bulfinch — the week’s copywork from the grammar booklet. One selection, two purposes.

4 · Dictation days only (grammar Days 3–4)

Parent, read to yourself, not aloud: “So we see at this day, whatever evils are abroad, hope never entirely leaves us.” — mark the 2–3 hardest words; the routine is on the Term 1 cover. Same sentence as the grammar booklet — one dictation, not two. On narration days the strip ends at step 3.

Year 6 · Term 1 — Teacher Answer Key

Keep this separate from the student booklet. Score Conventions on the child’s own pen only.

Week 1 · The Noun at Work
1a Prometheus=S, fire=DO; 1b jar=DO, door=OP.   2 hope=ABS · mankind=COL · firelight=CMP.   3 appositive "the wise Titan" renames Prometheus.
Week 2 · Pronouns &amp; Antecedents
1a he→Perseus, it→shield; 1b their→nymphs.   2 "Athena guided Perseus, and she warned him."   3 name the person, e.g., "…told the king that the king was a coward."
Week 3 · Verbs: Action vs. Linking
1 a=A, b=L, c=L.   2 prince (predicate noun); calm (predicate adjective).   3 any action, e.g., "The soldiers charged without fear."
Week 4 · Adjectives &amp; Articles
1 adj: tall, polished, crested; articles: A, the.   2 narrow→road, hot→road, endless→road.   3 any fitting pair, e.g., "the vast, groaning galley."
Week 5 · Adverbs — Manner, Time, Place
1 a=M, b=T, c=P.   2 the verb ruled.   3 any manner adverb, e.g., calmly, boldly.
Week 6 · Prepositions &amp; Their Objects
1 through/meadow, beyond/river, into/cave.   2 ground.   3 any where-phrase, e.g., "…searched across the whole earth."
Week 7 · Prepositional Phrases
1 [on the wide sea], [before a strong wind].   2 adjective (describes crown).   3 "At midnight" (adverbial, tells when); "from the tower" tells where.
Week 8 · Coordinating Conjunctions
1a and = words; 1b but = sentences.   2 e.g., "The chariot raced, and the crowd roared."   3 "…long, so they rested." (comma before so joining two sentences).
Week 9 · Subordinating Conjunctions
1a "Because the people trusted him"; 1b "until the storm passed."   2 No — it depends on the main clause to finish the thought.   3 e.g., When.
Week 10 · Appositive &amp; Participial Phrases
1 appositive "a proud Roman" renames Messala.   2 "Chained to the oar" (describes Judah).   3 any fitting renaming phrase, e.g., "a quiet stranger."
Week 11 · Parse a Sentence Fully
1 The(art) loyal(adj) servant(noun,S) quietly(adv) brought(verb) a(art) message(noun,DO) to(prep) the(art) queen(noun,OP).   2 phrases: none here except "to the queen"; subject=servant, verb=brought. (If you added an opener like "In the hall," bracket that too.)
Week 12 · Find Every Phrase
1 participial = "Standing on the deck"; appositive = "a brave sailor" (renames captain); prep. phrases = "on the deck," "with steady eyes."   2 subject captain, verb watched, DO storm.
Ready to advance when he can identify parts of speech, spot phrases, and name noun functions with growing ease — the ground the workbook's sentence analysis stands on. Score Conventions on his own pen only.
